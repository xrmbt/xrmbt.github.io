"Did you know that the fog is my favorite weather phenomenon?" He didn't answer. Mercy's beater Ford rattled along the concrete veins of the highway as it veered up, up, up to a cresting wave, the fog lifting---no, rather, the truck peeking up over the fog---for just a brief moment. A beautiful moon hung in the sky, and you could see the quaint showcase of blinding flourescent light emitting from the solitary, lonely gas station of Sonora in the distance, before the truck barrelled back down into the wispy grey void. He reached into his inner right jacket pocket, producing a pack of smokes, Elton Lite Select 250s, and coolly flipped it open, sliding out one cigarette in a mechanical manner as he had tens of thousands of times before, grasping it with his teeth as he slammed it shut and put it back, his other arm fumbling in his outer right jacket pocket until he had found the Zippo he had been looking for (jet black, no design). A click and strike later, he inhaled deeply as the flame met the end, then exhaled, crafting a similar phenomenon within the car as outside. "Can't you do that with the window rolled down at least?" "My car, my rules." "Hey, at least you finally said something." "Keep your mouth shut." A flickering streetlamp in the distance reminded her of David Lynch. The whole situation was a bit hard to be cognizant of.
